# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Single owner-operator investor managing personal capital in a private wealth operating system.
The user works across portfolio construction, DCA, dividend income, retirement planning, research, and decision-making.
This is a solo workflow, not a team dashboard, CRM, advisor workstation, or enterprise platform.

## Product Purpose
Be Blank OS helps a long-term investor keep sheet-backed holdings, allocation targets, dividend income, research, and action queues in one calm operating workspace.
The product exists to make the next good decision obvious while preserving the production sheet as the source of truth.
Success means the user can review current holdings, understand drift and income, queue actions, and stay confident that the UI reflects real data.

## Brand Personality
Private, disciplined, editorial.
The tone should feel calm, precise, and premium, with the confidence of a family-office tool built for a single serious investor.
The interface should reduce noise, support long-horizon thinking, and avoid the energy of a trading terminal or consumer finance app.

## Anti-references
- CRM-style dashboards with sales pipeline behavior
- Advisor workstations or multi-user collaboration tools
- Enterprise admin consoles with dense, generic tables
- Consumer finance apps with gamified nudges and clutter
- Startup SaaS dashboards with blue-heavy gradients and hero-metric clichés
- Trading terminals that emphasize speed and volatility over clarity

## Design Principles
1. Decision-first: the primary action should be obvious on each screen.
2. Source-of-truth clarity: sheet-backed holdings and records must remain visually trustworthy and easy to audit.
3. Calm investing: reduce noise, visual competition, and trading-floor urgency.
4. Long-term orientation: emphasize allocation, income, planning, and research over short-term movement.
5. Editorial hierarchy: use strong typographic structure, whitespace, and measured rhythm to make the workspace feel premium and legible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Standard WCAG-level accessibility is sufficient.
Priorities are clear typography, strong information hierarchy, keyboard navigation where practical, and reliable contrast ratios.
No high-contrast mode or special enterprise accessibility workflows are required.
